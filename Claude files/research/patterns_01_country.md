## Patterns

### 1. US domestic first-attempt auth is the global top quartile
- **Pattern**: US-issued card, US-acquired, USD CNP subscription auth rate sits near the ceiling for a given card type.
- **Value**: Domestic US first-attempt CNP auth ~87-89% on well-optimized stacks; industry "good" benchmark starts at ~85% (Checkout.com). Stripe Authorization Boost claims avg +1-2pp lift from this baseline.
- **Source**: Checkout.com [13], Stripe [2].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country, card_country, is_approved

### 2. India has the lowest card-mandate first-attempt auth in the 30-country set
- **Pattern**: RBI e-mandate framework plus AFA requirement above INR 15,000 drives structurally low approvals on card recurring; UPI AutoPay is lower still.
- **Value**: Card-mandate recurring success ~70% (Cashfree). UPI AutoPay collapsed from ~50% (Jan 2024) to ~30% by Nov 2025. Net blended India recurring auth ~50-65% vs US ~87-89% — 25-35pp spread.
- **Source**: Cashfree [24], Whalesbook [26], Prove [27].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country, payment_method_type, is_approved, decline_category

### 3. Nordics cluster at the top of European auth rates
- **Pattern**: SE, DK, FI, NO have near-universal card penetration, high contactless, low fraud — translating to high CNP first-attempt auth.
- **Value**: Industry-estimate first-attempt CNP auth 88-92% (SE/DK/FI) and 86-90% (NO, because BankAxept routing forces some cross-scheme), vs pan-EU average ~82-85%.
- **Source**: Estimate based on NORBr Nordics guide and Checkout.com benchmark [13]; NORBr article.
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country, is_approved

### 3a. Japan 3DS mandate cut CNP completion by ~19pp
- **Pattern**: Japan's JCA EMV 3DS mandate (effective 1 Apr 2025) shifted approvals from low-friction to challenge-heavy.
- **Value**: 3DS completion ~75% vs non-3DS 94% — a 19pp completion drop in the first month of enforcement. Europe comparable SCA drop-off is 20-25%.
- **Source**: Forter [16], Adyen [10], MRC [48].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country, is_approved, decline_category (sca_required / auth_failed)

### 4. Korea is domestic-rail dominant — international cards see higher declines
- **Pattern**: Most Korean CNP volume routes through domestic issuers (BC Card, Shinhan) with local authentication requirements; foreign-issuer cards face elevated decline.
- **Value**: Estimated first-attempt CNP auth 85-88% for KR-issued on KR acquirer; cross-border (foreign acquirer) falls to 70-78% — ~10-15pp drag.
- **Source**: Estimate based on general APAC local-acquiring advantage (BlueSnap [15]: 10-13% uplift from local acquiring cross-border).
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country, card_country, is_approved

### 5. Cross-border acquiring costs 10-13pp in approval rate
- **Pattern**: Card-country ≠ acquirer-country results in meaningful decline-rate drag regardless of market pair.
- **Value**: BlueSnap benchmark: cross-border auth lift from local acquiring 10-13pp vs 1-3pp domestic uplift. Translates to ~1000-1300 bps auth drag on cross-border.
- **Source**: BlueSnap [15].
- **Applies to column(s)**: card_country, customer_country, is_approved

### 6. USD billing outside the US drops auth materially
- **Pattern**: Non-US customer paying in USD triggers foreign-currency / cross-border flags at issuer; rate depends on issuer's international risk model.
- **Value**: Industry estimate: 300-800 bps auth drag for USD-billed vs local-currency-billed in EU/UK; 1000-1500 bps in LATAM/TR/AR/IN. Visa cross-border fee is 80 bps (USD-settled) vs 120 bps (non-USD settled), reflecting issuer risk pricing.
- **Source**: Estimate; Rapyd cross-border fees [44]; BlueSnap [15].
- **Applies to column(s)**: currency, customer_country, card_country, is_approved

### 7. Netherlands iDEAL dominates — card share is residual
- **Pattern**: iDEAL carries the bulk of Dutch ecommerce; cards are secondary and skew corporate.
- **Value**: iDEAL ~70-73% of Dutch online purchases; 1.47B iDEAL txns in 2024. Cards ~15-20% share.
- **Source**: Statista [30], Cross-Border Mag [31].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=NL, payment_method_type

### 8. Germany SEPA DD penetration is the highest in the EU for subscriptions
- **Pattern**: Low credit-card penetration (<40%) makes SEPA DD the default subscription rail.
- **Value**: In DE, ~50% of recurring subscription payments via SEPA DD; DE + NL card penetration <50% — SlimPay/Adyen characterize as the archetypal DD markets. SEPA DD failure rate 0.5-3% (Adyen-cited).
- **Source**: Adyen [11], SlimPay guide.
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=DE/NL, payment_method_type, is_approved

### 9. Belgium Bancontact is near-iDEAL dominance
- **Pattern**: Bancontact + Payconiq are the default consumer rail; international cards under-represented.
- **Value**: ~60% of Belgian consumer online purchases via Bancontact; 2.5B combined Bancontact+Payconiq txns in 2024.
- **Source**: KVK / NORBr (via search summary).
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=BE, payment_method_type

### 10. Poland BLIK is #1 ecommerce method in 2024
- **Pattern**: BLIK overtook quick bank-transfers and cards in 2024 as the preferred online payment method.
- **Value**: BLIK 68% preference share for online shopping (Gemius 2024); 86% of 15-24yo use it; 2.4B BLIK txns in 2024; BLIK >50% of PL ecomm by txn count. Cards ~43% preference (a shopper can pick multiple).
- **Source**: Gemius [29], BLIK [28].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=PL, payment_method_type

### 11. Switzerland TWINT covers the majority of merchants
- **Pattern**: TWINT is the local wallet standard; cards and TWINT co-exist.
- **Value**: 5M active TWINT users (~55% of Swiss adults); >75% of merchants accept; 386M txns/yr. Cards still ~52% of online txns — TWINT growing share.
- **Source**: Adyen [12], Wikipedia TWINT [43].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=CH, payment_method_type

### 12. Brazil — card still dominant but PIX Automatico disrupting from Jun 2025
- **Pattern**: Cards were majority of Brazilian CNP subscriptions until June 2025; Pix Automático now attacks the 60M un-carded consumers.
- **Value**: PIX overall: 40% of Brazil ecomm value (projected 51% by 2027); cards projected to fall from 57% (2023) to 36% by 2027. Pix Automático live Jun 16, 2025.
- **Source**: PPRO [32], MEF [33], Worldpay GPR [18].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=BR, payment_method_type

### 13. Mexico — cash voucher (OXXO) material even for streaming subscriptions
- **Pattern**: ~15% of streaming-service payment-method share in Mexico is OXXO Pay voucher — unique for a recurring-billing market.
- **Value**: OXXO Pay ~15% share of streaming; SPEI ~3M txns/day; cards still majority but cash-based ~11% of LATAM ecomm value.
- **Source**: Nuvei [35], PaymentsCMI MX [34], Worldpay [18].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=MX, payment_method_type

### 14. Argentina — capital controls lifted Apr 2025; card+USD now works again
- **Pattern**: Pre-Apr 2025, Argentine card-issued USD subscription auth was crushed by cepo / limits; post-reform, gap with MEP/official/blue rates collapsed.
- **Value**: Pre-reform USD subscription approval in AR est. <40%; post-reform estimate 70-80%. All three rates now ARS 1,430-1,460/USD (within ~2% of each other).
- **Source**: Real Instituto Elcano [40].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=AR, currency, is_approved

### 15. Turkey — Turkish cards frequently blocked for non-TRY CNP
- **Pattern**: Turkish issuers and regulation push volume toward TRY; USD/EUR subscription requires explicit "trusted merchant" enablement per cardholder.
- **Value**: Industry estimate: Turkish-issued cards on USD-billed subscription auth ~55-65% first-attempt vs ~85% on TRY-billed. FX control + bank-level merchant whitelist friction.
- **Source**: Stripe TR [7], estimate.
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=TR, currency, is_approved

### 16. Russia — cross-border card ecosystem effectively closed
- **Pattern**: Visa/Mastercard suspended Mar 2022; RU-issued V/MC cards do not work abroad; foreign-issued V/MC cards do not work in RU.
- **Value**: ~0% approval on RU-issued cards for foreign subscription; Mir share ~66.7% of domestic card txns in 2024; 400M+ Mir cards issued cumulative.
- **Source**: Mastercard [41], Mir [42].
- **Applies to column(s)**: card_country=RU, customer_country=RU, is_approved

### 17. Nigeria — naira-denominated cards largely disabled for foreign CNP
- **Pattern**: CBN FX scarcity drove major Nigerian banks to suspend international card usage; limits went from $50K/yr (2015) to $20/month (2022) to effectively $0.
- **Value**: >50% of electronic payment failures in NG in 2023 involved cross-border (CBN); foreign subscription auth on NG naira cards estimated <15%. Recent (2024-25) partial re-enablement.
- **Source**: BusinessDay NG [38], Techpoint [39].
- **Applies to column(s)**: card_country=NG, currency, is_approved, decline_category

### 18. Egypt — USD subscription auth structurally drag by FX restrictions
- **Pattern**: Central Bank of Egypt imposes periodic USD limits on cards; subscription USD billing sees elevated soft declines.
- **Value**: Industry estimate: EG-issued on USD subscription auth 50-65% first-attempt; Egyptian pound depreciation (>50% in 2022-24) amplifies do-not-honor declines. Worldpay: MEA cards 47% of ecomm, cash on delivery 17%.
- **Source**: Estimate; Worldpay GPR MEA [18].
- **Applies to column(s)**: card_country=EG, currency, is_approved

### 19. Indonesia — high fraud concentration, elevated decline
- **Pattern**: Sumsub flags ID as a global fraud hotspot for ecommerce.
- **Value**: ~35% of ID ecomm transactions flagged fraudulent in prior studies (builderfly); Sumsub 2024 Fraudlympics: Nigeria #1, China #2, Indonesia #3. Fraud-decline category materially higher.
- **Source**: Sumsub [37].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=ID, decline_category=fraud

### 20. South Africa — fraud rate ~10× world average
- **Pattern**: ZA ecomm fraud dwarfs global baseline; issuers reflect this in conservative CNP approvals.
- **Value**: ~25% of ZA ecomm transactions flagged fraudulent (~10× global avg). Likely translates to 500-1000 bps auth drag vs a "clean" market.
- **Source**: OPMC / techinafrica (search summary).
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=ZA, decline_category=fraud, is_approved

### 21. Brazil — highest credit-card fraud rate globally; elevated chargebacks
- **Pattern**: Brazil records 3-6 fraudulent txns/minute; chargeback rate ~3.55% (~355 bps) vs ~1% scheme threshold.
- **Value**: BR chargeback rate 355 bps; MX 282 bps. Chargeback-to-sale ratio in BR triple the scheme threshold.
- **Source**: Alphacomm (search summary).
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=BR, chargeback, decline_category=fraud

### 22. Insufficient_funds is the #1 decline code globally — ~44% share
- **Pattern**: Universal: across markets, insufficient_funds / hard decline variant is the modal reason, though concentration varies.
- **Value**: 44% of declines are insufficient_funds (Ethoca/Chargebacks911). Higher in low-income high-volatility markets (AR, TR, NG, EG est. 55-65%); lower in prime US/UK/Nordics (est. 30-38%).
- **Source**: Chargebacks911 [22], Stripe codes [3].
- **Applies to column(s)**: decline_category=insufficient_funds, customer_country

### 23. Do_not_honor is the catch-all — concentrated in LATAM/TR/emerging
- **Pattern**: Do_not_honor (generic issuer decline) indexes soft-decline churn where issuer doesn't surface reason.
- **Value**: Estimated share of declines: ~15-20% in US/UK/EU prime; 30-45% in BR/AR/TR/IN because issuers prefer generic code over specific. Retry-friendly code.
- **Source**: Stripe [3], DirectPayNet (search summary), estimate.
- **Applies to column(s)**: decline_category=do_not_honor, customer_country

### 24. SCA challenge drop-off = ~20-25pp in Europe
- **Pattern**: PSD2 SCA in EU/UK forces 3DS2 on non-exempt txns; challenge flows lose ~1 in 5 customers.
- **Value**: 20-25% drop-off on 3DS-challenged txns (Adyen, Ravelin); TRA exemption + low-value <30 EUR exemption reduce this. EU average first-attempt CNP auth ~82-85% net of SCA.
- **Source**: Adyen [10], Ravelin [17], GPayments [49].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country in EU/UK, is_approved, decline_category=sca_required

### 25. UK — prime issuer mix, best EU auth rates after Nordics
- **Pattern**: GB has high card penetration, low fraud, low cross-border exposure for domestic subs.
- **Value**: UK first-attempt CNP auth estimate 85-88%; SCA challenge rate lower than EU average due to broader TRA use. UK contactless >90% POS.
- **Source**: Estimate; GPayments [49].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=GB, is_approved

### 26. France — authorisation drag from conservative issuer model + SCA
- **Pattern**: French issuers (BNP, Credit Agricole, LCL) tend to challenge more; CB scheme + Visa/MC co-badging introduces routing quirks.
- **Value**: FR first-attempt CNP auth estimate 80-84%; ~3-5pp below UK. Checkout.com reported +8.99% AR uplift specifically in FR via optimization — indicates low baseline.
- **Source**: Checkout.com [14], estimate.
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=FR, is_approved

### 27. Italy / Spain / Portugal — South-EU mid-tier; card growing on prepaid
- **Pattern**: High prepaid card share (Postepay in IT, many Santander prepaid in ES) — prepaid often has stricter CNP rules.
- **Value**: IT/ES/PT first-attempt CNP auth estimate 78-84%; prepaid cards decline ~3-5pp more than credit on subscription due to balance volatility.
- **Source**: Estimate; Stripe analytics framework [1].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country in IT/ES/PT, card_funding_type=prepaid, is_approved

### 28. Canada — US-like auth on credit, SEPA-DD-like rail missing
- **Pattern**: Canadian CNP behaves similarly to US; Interac e-Transfer not standardized for recurring subs; heavy reliance on credit card.
- **Value**: CA first-attempt CNP auth estimate 86-89% (near US). Credit cards 46% of ecomm, 50% of POS (Worldpay 2024).
- **Source**: Worldpay GPR [18].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=CA, is_approved, payment_method_type

### 29. Australia — strong card auth but NPP/PayID growing fast for sub billing
- **Pattern**: AU CNP auth high; PayTo (PayID+NPP) now a direct-debit alternative with mandate model.
- **Value**: AU first-attempt CNP auth estimate 85-88%; 25M+ PayIDs registered; NPP >35% of A2A transactions. PayTo positioned to replace BECS for sub mandates.
- **Source**: PaymentsCMI AU [36].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=AU, is_approved, payment_method_type

### 30. Singapore & Hong Kong — high auth, high cross-border exposure
- **Pattern**: Financial-hub markets: very high quality issuers, but large share of volume is cross-border (expat / multi-currency).
- **Value**: SG/HK domestic-domestic CNP auth estimate 86-90%; cross-border (foreign merchant) 75-82%. HK issuer credit tightening 2025 (low delinquency, fewer revolvers) suggests issuer-side decline tightening.
- **Source**: Estimate; GlobalData HK market (search).
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=SG/HK, is_approved, card_country vs merchant_country

### 31. ACH (US) — subscription failure rate ~2.7-5% vs card 15%
- **Pattern**: ACH bank debit in the US has materially lower subscription failure rate than cards because bank accounts don't expire.
- **Value**: ACH direct debit failure 2.7-5% (Ordway, GoCardless); card recurring failure ~15% (Recurly). 10-12pp gap. Translates directly to lower involuntary churn in ACH-enrolled US cohorts.
- **Source**: Ordway [45], Recurly [20], Monetizely [47].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country=US, payment_method_type=ach, is_approved

### 32. Recurring card decline ~15% average — the global SaaS baseline
- **Pattern**: Across subscription rails, ~15% of recurring card attempts fail; 85%+ rail is top-quartile.
- **Value**: Average recurring card failure ~15% (Recurly); top SaaS recovery 73-85% of at-risk. 20-40% of churn is involuntary.
- **Source**: Recurly [20][21].
- **Applies to column(s)**: is_approved, decline_category, retry_attempt_number

### 33. Dunning + retry recovers 45-73% of failed subs — recovery curve by country
- **Pattern**: Recovery effectiveness depends on country issuer behavior and decline mix.
- **Value**: 45-70% recovery via optimized retries (Churn Buster); Recurly 73% at-risk rescue. Recovery materially better in US/EU prime (60-75%) than IN/TR/NG/AR (20-40%) because hard-decline share higher in emerging.
- **Source**: Recurly [20], Monetizely [47].
- **Applies to column(s)**: is_approved, retry_attempt_number, customer_country

### 34. Weekday / monthly seasonality — Mondays and 1st-of-month up
- **Pattern**: Subscription auth rates rise after payday; higher balances → fewer insufficient_funds declines.
- **Value**: Industry estimate: +100-300 bps approval on 1st-5th of month vs 25th-30th. US/EU paycheck effect weaker than BR/MX/IN where monthly salaries land on specific dates. Mondays +50-150 bps vs Sundays (issuer staffing + customer awareness).
- **Source**: Estimate based on Recurly dunning guidance [20].
- **Applies to column(s)**: attempt_timestamp (day_of_month, day_of_week), is_approved

### 35. Ramadan in MENA markets — subscription churn and auth dip
- **Pattern**: Ramadan period in TR/EG (and partially ID with its Muslim-majority) correlates with spending-pattern shift — higher utility/charity, lower discretionary sub spend; issuers also see spike in spending that can exhaust balances mid-month.
- **Value**: Industry estimate: -200 to -400 bps first-attempt auth during Ramadan in TR/EG/ID on non-essential subs; voluntary churn +50-150 bps. Eid week spike in fraud declines.
- **Source**: Estimate.
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country in TR/EG/ID, attempt_timestamp (month), is_approved

### 36. Neobank / challenger card auth materially weaker than prime in EU
- **Pattern**: N26, Revolut, Monzo, Wise, bunq etc. issuers have different risk profiles; can apply more aggressive limits for CNP subscriptions.
- **Value**: Industry estimate: neobank-issued cards first-attempt CNP auth 75-82% vs prime EU issuers 85-88% — 500-1000 bps gap. Highest in DE/IE/GB where neobanks have material share.
- **Source**: Estimate; Stripe issuer behavior analytics [1].
- **Applies to column(s)**: card_funding_type, issuer_type, customer_country, is_approved

### 37. Widest prime/mid/neobank spread — UK, Ireland, Germany
- **Pattern**: Markets with high neobank penetration have the widest issuer-quality spread affecting subscription auth.
- **Value**: GB (Monzo, Revolut, Starling ~20-25% of accounts), IE (Revolut >50% of adults), DE (N26, bunq). Prime-vs-neobank auth spread estimate: GB ~8pp, IE ~10pp, DE ~7pp. Compare US where prime issuers dominate — spread <4pp.
- **Source**: Estimate.
- **Applies to column(s)**: issuer_type, customer_country, is_approved

### 38. Token / network token lift — 5-15pp reduction in expired-card declines
- **Pattern**: Visa/MC Account Updater + network tokens auto-update credentials, cutting lifecycle declines.
- **Value**: Tokenization reduces expired/reissued-card declines by 5-15% (Spreedly/industry). Higher adoption in US/UK/Nordics (~70%+ issuer coverage) than BR/TR/IN (<40%).
- **Source**: Spreedly (search), Stripe [2].
- **Applies to column(s)**: is_tokenized, decline_category=expired_card, customer_country

### 39. Colombia and Chile — mid-tier LATAM; cards OK for CNP subs
- **Pattern**: CO and CL have better-regulated card systems than AR; both process international CNP relatively cleanly vs regional peers.
- **Value**: CO/CL first-attempt CNP auth estimate 75-82% — 5-10pp below US but 10-15pp above AR. Chargeback rates lower than BR/MX. USD subscription works (no cepo equivalent).
- **Source**: Estimate; Worldpay LATAM [18].
- **Applies to column(s)**: customer_country in CO/CL, is_approved

### 40. Czech Republic — card penetration high, CNP auth at west-EU level
- **Pattern**: CZ (and to lesser extent PL for card) behaves like west-EU for CNP; low fraud, high approval.
